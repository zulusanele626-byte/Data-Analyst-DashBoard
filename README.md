# Data Professional Survey Dashboard (Power BI)-

objective
To analyze bike sales data and identify key factors influencing customer purchasing behavior using interactive dashboards, enabling data-driven insights based on income, age, and commute distance.

data set used :

as Questions (KPIS):

-Total Bikes Purchased – Number of customers who bought bikes.

-Purchase Rate (%) – Percentage of customers who purchased bikes.

-Average Income per Customer – Income comparison between buyers and non-buyers.

-Customer Age Group Distribution – Purchases by age brackets.

-Purchase by Commute Distance – Buying trends based on distance to work.

-Gender-based Purchase Rate – Comparison of male vs female buyers.

-Impact of Cars Owned – How car ownership affects bike purchases.

-Home Ownership Influence – Buying patterns based on home ownership.

#Process

Data Analysis – Explored relationships between income, age, gender, and purchases.

#PROJECT INSIGHT

Customers with higher average income are more likely to purchase bikes.
Middle-aged individuals show the highest purchase rate compared to other age groups.
Shorter commute distances are associated with higher bike purchases.
Males slightly outperform females in purchase rate, though the gap is not significant.
Customers with fewer cars are more likely to buy bikes, suggesting substitution for transport.
Home ownership and marital status also influence purchasing patterns, revealing specific target segments.


#CONCLUSION

The analysis shows that income, age, and commute distance are key drivers of bike purchases. Customers with higher income, shorter commutes, and fewer cars are more inclined to buy bikes. These insights can help businesses better target their ideal customers and improve marketing and sales strategies.
